PARATTA

Paratha is a flaky, pan-fried flatbread from the Indian subcontinent, made from whole wheat flour. It can be served plain or stuffed with fillings like potatoes or paneer and is often enjoyed with yogurt, pickles, or curry.

    • POORI

    Poori is soft, making it a popular choice for breakfast or festive meals.The soft, deep-fried poori is paired with a flavorful, spiced chickpea curry (kadalai curry), creating a delicious combination often enjoyed for breakfast or lunch.

    • VADAI & SHAMPOL

    Vadai with Sampol is a savory snack combo from South India. made crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. Sampol is a spicy, tangy coconut relish or chutney, typically served as a dipping sauce, adding flavor and zest to the crispy vadai.

    • STRING HOPPERS

    String Hoppers (Idiyappam) are delicate, steamed noodles made from rice flour. They are soft and airy, often served with curry, coconut milk, or chutney. A staple in South India and Sri Lanka, they are commonly enjoyed for breakfast or dinner. Their light texture and versatility make them a favorite in many households.

    • BRIYANI

    Biryani is a fragrant rice dish made with basmati rice, spices, and meat, or sometimes vegetables. The rice is layered with marinated meat and cooked with aromatic spices like saffron, cardamom, and cloves. It's often garnished with fried onions and boiled eggs. Biryani is a popular and festive dish, especially in South Asia.

    • CHICKEN FRY

    Chicken is a widely consumed poultry meat known for its versatility and mild flavor. It can be cooked in various ways such as grilling, frying, roasting, or boiling, and is used in countless dishes across the world. Chicken is a rich source of protein, often paired with vegetables, grains, or spices for a balanced meal.
